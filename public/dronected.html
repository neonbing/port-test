Problem

Drones are an increasingly vital tool for search and rescue(SAR), but they still have serious limitations. They are error prone and time consuming manual video analysis, limitation by terrain blocking signals and extremely high cost to upgrade.

Solution

Our project seeks to allow a remote cooperative workflow and crowdsourced upgrades for existing search and rescue drones using the high-bandwidth of 5G networks. The solution includes a website allows more than more eyes to monitor the drone video feed without location limitation and reduce on-site pilot‚Äôs cognitive load along with cloud AI algorithm and an add-on module that is compatible with different drone models

Outcome

Our prototype and use case will be presented in T-Mobile Tech Experience Center as a part of the 5G showcase.

Process

EXPLORATORY RESEARCH

Drones have serious limitations, including inefficient manual video analysis, unstable radio signals and high cost to upgrade

New technologies such as drones expand search and rescue (SAR)‚Äôs ability to locate people, but there are some limitations. We conducted 15 interviews with SAR team members and rescuees who have been rescued before to understand the workflow and dig out pain points.
These three pain points were mentioned over and over again by SAR teams in the interviews and we organized them.

  • Spotting a person on a tiny screen mounted on the controller is very difficult, not to mention controlling the drone at the same time.

  • Advanced functionality on the drone is expensive for most search and rescue organizations.

  • Radio communication with drones can be choppy with the rough terrain present in many search and rescue scenarios.

    • So how can we present a reasonably priced drone product to increase the effectiveness of spotting subjects without radio limitation?

    USER CASE

    DESIGN PRINCIPLES

    Define our product in a balance of different stakeholders

    Before jumping into ideation, engineers, business managers and designers in our team discussed and set this design criteria together to achieve the balance between different aspects of the product.

      [Desirability] Reduce cognitive load on the pilots

      [Desirability] Increase the search effectiveness

      [Viability] Affordable for volunteer teams

      [Desirability] Not exclude any possibilities of saving a life

      [Desirability] Easy to set up on existing drones

      [Viability] Apply to the majority of structures of SAR team

      [Desirability] Will not decrease the flight time more than 30%

      [Feasibility] Able to develop in 3 months

    PRIORITISATION

    From requirements to features

    We ideated possible solutions to solve this problem, like radio relay, cellular connection and 2-way voice communication. Given our time limitations, how can we put our effort into the most valuable area?

    In order to prioritize our features, we made a 3-axis matrix and picked the features that are most useful for SAR and T-Mobile which we are able to develop.

    SOLUTION

    A collaborative work system with cloud-based AI assistance and flexible 5G-connected module with dual cameras on existing drones

    By connecting drones to the cellular network, data is no longer limited in small area. That means other remote pilots, or even algorithms can review the video data and find insights to boost the efficiency of search. In addition, cellular networks will be less likely to be blocked by terrains like hills and make beyond-line-of-sight missions possible.

  • Cellular control for beyond-line-of-sight missions

  • Cloud-based objection detection AI

  • Remote collaboration speeds up spotting subjects

    • The integrated product solution includes an add-on module attached to the drone and cloud powered website enabling real-time video streaming, remote collaboration and machine learning assistance.

    test & iteration

    Test in fields and get insights to iterate on prototype

    We had two rounds of field tests, one with non-SAR participants and another one with SAR pilots and iterated our prototype twice. Here are highlights of what we learned.

  • Humans have limited attention and working memory resources, so adding an object detection algorithm is necessary to provide more comprehensive information to assist people.
  • Remote co-pilot lack the in-field information to make judgements. Thus, adding radar, drone direction and speed is necessary for them to make better decisions.
  • Well trained SAR professionals are able to communicate efficiently, so verbal communication is more suitable for them.

    • Other than that, we improved the learnability and efficiency of the system and designed a desktop website for remote co-pilots and a mobile website for on-site pilots.

    Actionable insights
  • Communication between pilot and co-pilot needs to be more intuitive as 3 out of 6 users spontaneously talk to pilots: e.g. ‚ÄúCan you fly closer to those two people?‚Äù. Thus,we need to embed audio chat on the top of the pin drop communication.
  • Make the feedback and drone work status more transparent to pilots, especially co-pilots who are not in the field. Co-pilots always raise confusions like if they lost the Internet or the gimbal is broken when they can‚Äôt adjust view by pressing keys.
  • Algorithms are necessary to help detect subjects in real-time video inspection as 5 out of 6 people missed catching people just because their attention happened to be focused on other areas in the image.

    Design Guide

    Final Deliverable

    Let‚Äôs walk through how the multi-pilots collaboration system with drone works

    #1 Speedy and informative onboarding stage to let remote pilots prepare for the mission

    When receiving the task, the remote pilot logs in the system, and takes a glance of the mission. If the remote pilot is the first time to join the mission, they will receive a short training to get familiar with the system.

    #2 Efficient information display, minimalied operation and controls, fully focused on video feed and providing users who are not controlling the drone the necessary information of context.

    In user testing, miscommunication and low efficient conversations happened a lot because remote co-pilots are not able to understand the status of drones, like directions of both drone and camera.

    #3 Voice call over the website and preference settings to suit various situations

    The website enables two-way communication between on-site pilots and remote co-pilots. Under different scenarios or according to preferences, users can change the layout, resolution, change the palette color of thermal camera and mark pin on map.

    In our user test, pilots voiced that when exploring, they prefer lower resolution and lower latency, but while the drone is hovering, they prefer higher resolution. Also, some pilots prefer the rainbow HC palette, some prefer the gray scale.

    #4 The website allows pilots to record video and pictures and automatically record the coordinates to spread information to the ground team and for evidence.

    In our user research, pilots all mentioned that they need to record the GPS coordinates and images of people to guide ground teams and sometimes leave records for evidence purpose.

    #5 Turn on object detection machine learning help pilots to better spot people and clues(This is live!)

    In our user test, we found that people struggled with noticing flashing and subtle evidence because of limited attention resources. Object detection algorithms can assist with that, highlighting the interest spot for pilots and then they can make judgement on it.

    What‚Äôs next

    1. Collect data and train machine learning model
    We use the Yolo v5 model with a public dataset which is good, but not ideal for drones. We will collect 50,000+ annotated images for training the neural network to increase the accuracy under different weather situations and terrains.
    2. Research on the optimal threshold and the information display styles of AI
    There is no end to improving AI performance, so we need to determine the best breakpoint which is useful enough for SAR and also not too hard to get. We will have a set of experiments to figure out what‚Äôs the best error rate and accuracy rate of the algorithms to boost search efficiency and lower cognitive load.
