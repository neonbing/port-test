Problem

Drones are an increasingly vital tool for search and rescue (SAR), but they have severe limitations.

  • Spotting people on a tiny screen mounted on the drone controller is challenging, not to mention controlling the drone simultaneously.

  • Upgrading drones entirely for advanced functionality is expensive for most search and rescue organizations.

  • Radio communication with drones can be choppy, with rough terrain present in many search and rescue scenarios.

    • Solution

    Our project seeks to allow a remote cooperative workflow with versatile add-on modules for existing drones using high-bandwidth 5G networks.

    The website allows more than one person to monitor the drone video feed without location limitations and reduces the on-site pilot's cognitive load using AI.

    Microprocessors and cellular boards are added on ready-made drones ‚Äî no need to purchase a brand new drone.

    Outcome

    Our prototype and use case will be presented in T-Mobile Tech Experience Center as a part of the 5G showcase.

    Insight #1

    Important clues are ignored because of high cognitive workload and tiny screens

    6 pilots mentioned that they had a hard time spotting people on mobile phones while on-site and 3 of them had the experience that they found the missing person when they reviewed the video recording of drones on TV.
    Thus, our solution allow remote pilots to join the search by monitoring on big screens

    Insight #2

    Time is valuable due to the unknown rescuee‚Äôs safety situations and limited drone battery life

    Thus, we set increasing search efficiency and efficacy as our standard. Also, this insight led to two of our design principles.
    [Desirability] Does not decrease the flight time by more than 30%
    [Desirability] Focus on task and eliminate unnecessary steps

    Insight #3

    The principle is to not exclude any possibilities of saving a life

    Thus, we explore new technologies which will increase the likelihood to find missing persons.

    SOLUTION

    A collaborative work system with cloud-based AI assistant and flexible 5G-connected module with dual cameras on existing drones

    By connecting drones to the cellular network, data is no longer limited. That means other remote pilots or even algorithms can review the video data and find insights to boost search efficiency. Besides, cellular networks will be less likely to be blocked by terrains like hills and make beyond-line-of-sight missions possible.

    test & iteration

    Test in fields and get insights to iterate on prototype

    We had two rounds of field tests, one with non-SAR participants and another one with SAR pilots, and iterated our prototype twice.

  • Humans have limited attention and working memory resources, so adding an object detection algorithm is necessary to provide more comprehensive information to assist people.
  • Well-trained SAR professionals can communicate efficiently, so verbal communication is more suitable for them. Thus, we added a call feature embedded on our website.
  • Remote co-pilot lack the in-field information to make judgments.
    • We added more flight information so that co-pilots can immediately understand the drone‚Äôs status even though they are not on the spot. The new layout also utilizes more space to display the most critical streaming video.

    Actionable insights
  • Communication between pilot and co-pilot needs to be more intuitive as 3 out of 6 users spontaneously talk to pilots: e.g. ‚ÄúCan you fly closer to those two people?‚Äù. Thus,we need to embed audio chat on the top of the pin drop communication.
  • Make the feedback and drone work status more transparent to pilots, especially co-pilots who are not in the field. Co-pilots always raise confusions like if they lost the Internet or the gimbal is broken when they can‚Äôt adjust view by pressing keys.
  • Algorithms are necessary to help detect subjects in real-time video inspection as 5 out of 6 people missed catching people just because their attention happened to be focused on other areas in the image.

    Final Deliverable

    Let's walk through how the multi-pilots collaboration system with drone works.

    On-site pilots create a new task and connect the drone for task execution

    Remote co-pilots join the operation

    The information displayed is entirely focused on the video feed and provides remote co-pilots with context information.

    Pilots and co-pilots can join the voice call over the website for efficient communication

    Pilots and co-pilots can change the layout and resolution under different scenarios or preferences

    In our user test, pilots voiced that they prefer lower resolution and lower latency when the drone is moving, but while the drone is hovering, they prefer higher resolution.

    Pilots can record videos and pictures with coordinate records to transmit information to the ground team

    In our user research, pilots all mentioned that they need to log the GCS coordinates and images to guide ground teams and sometimes leave records for evidence purposes.

    Turn on object detection algorithm to help pilots spot people and clues faster(This is live!)

    Our user test found that people struggled with noticing flashing and subtle evidence due to limited attention resources. Machine learning can assist with that, highlighting points of interest for pilots.

    What‚Äôs next

    1. Collect data and train machine learning model
    We use the Yolo v5 model with a public dataset which is good, but not ideal for drones. We will collect 50,000+ annotated images for training the neural network to increase the accuracy under different weather situations and terrains.

    2. Research on the optimal threshold and the information display styles of AI
    There is no end to improving AI performance, so we need to determine the best breakpoint that is useful enough for SAR. We will have a set of experiments to figure out the best error rate and accuracy rate of the algorithms to boost search efficiency and lower cognitive load.

    Reflection

    1. Focus on the minimum viable product
    It is tempting to design more features and come up with solutions to non tested problems. However, it is vital to get the first version of the product out as soon as possible to understand users and iterate.

    2. Involving engineers in the design process will help the team be on the same page.
    I invited engineers to participate in interviews, discuss evaluation plans, observe and analyze research results. They can get striking and fresh information directly from users and feel ownership over the research results. That way, I don't need to spend more effort convincing them to adopt ideas.

    3. Always research and evaluate while designing and developing.
    Whenever we have some conflicted areas or challenges, we will seek help by consulting with experts or validating with users. As designers or developers, we always try to defend our work, so it's necessary to test it with real users.
