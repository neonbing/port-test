WORK ABOUT

Chronic migraine puts a lot of pressure and anxiety on patients, so patients are desperate for treatments. TheraView provides daily at-home VR-based biofeedback therapy to alleviate pain in the long term and motivate patients to keep managing chronic migraines.

Time:

January 2021 - February 2021

Role:

Mobie UX design, User research and User Evaluation

Team:

TheraView phone application is my solo design project based a group project that I participated in with Frank Zhou and Robin Yang

Problem

Biofeedback is an effective way to prevent migraines, but patients lack easy access to it and find it hard to keep practicing frequently.

People who suffer from chronic migraines can't manage their everyday daily life. Pain killer doesn't always help, and preventive medication costs a patient over 6,000 dollars a year. We researched an effective preventive non-medicine treatment, VR-biofeedback, using the Oculus Go device.

In the 12-week study with 50 chronic migraine patients in randomized groups using this device daily, we found that patients forgot to practice or lose motivation. And some of them want to keep using it but are reluctant to buy VR headsets just for the therapy.

How might we make VR-biofeedback therapy more ubiquitous, and how might we motivate chronic patients to stick to biofeedback training every day at home?

Solution

An at-home mobile VR biofeedback therapy with data tracking and analysis to log patients' progress

Engaging users in the onboarding session with easy-to-digest information bite and questions

Thorough tutorial to cover all steps in the process to reduce uneasiness

Attracting and immersive home page to attract users to start and easy to follow setup with hardware

Effective biofeedback therapy and timely feedback and data log to strengthen users' belief

Interpret the complicated terms and data in plain words for users, so they will fully acknowledge the progress

Prototype and User Test

I tested the prototype with 3 users, using the usability test with think-aloud protocol to go through the whole process. The main research problems are: How easy is it to learn biofeedback techniques and practice procedures? How easy is it to understand tracking data, and how valuable is the data?

  • Users feel lost in the onboarding setup because of a lack of context and explanation. Thus, I break down the whole setup procedure into chunks, provide enough information for users to choose from, and always provide the "come back later" option.

  • It takes a while for users to understand the tracking data graph, and they wonâ€™t go too deep into figuring it out. Thus, I add glanceable reviews for users to digest quickly.

  • Users are very familiar with standard interfaces so they always take a glance and then take actions quickly rather than reading every word on the interface. Thus, I provide vital information more than once in the process.
