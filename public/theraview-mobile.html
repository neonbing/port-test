WORK ABOUT

TheraView provides chronic migraine patients with mobile VR biofeedback therapy and tracks their therapy progress and helps them to build a habit of practicing biofeedback.

Time:

January 2021 - February 2021

Role:

Mobie UX design and UX research

Team:

This is a sole design project followed a group project that I participated in with Frank Zhou and Robin Yang

Problem

People who suffer from chronic migraine can’t manage their normal daily life and lose the ability to work or live normally. Pain killer doesn’t always help and preventive medication costs a patient over 6000 dollars a year. We developed an effective preventive non-medicine treatment, VR-biofeedback using Oculus Go VR headset.

In the 8-week study with 50 chronic migraine patients in randomized groups using this device daily, we found that people may forget to practice or lose motivation. And people want to keep using it but are reluctant to buy VR headsets just for this therapy. So how might we make VR-biofeedback therapy more ubiquitous and motive patients to keep practicing?

USER RESEARCH

Understand user needs and narrow key themes for product exploration

Key Themes

  • Coping with chronic migraine is a long journey, which needs a lot of patience, encouragement and confidence.
  • Understanding the mechanisms of therapy will help patients to behave better in therapy.

    User Flow

    Low-fidelity user flow from onboarding to tracking data

    #1 Onboarding and setup

    Most users may not understand what biofeedback is, so the onboarding process will introduce why biofeedback and how to practice. After logging in, set up a scene and device to let users get familiar with how the therapy works and set up a reminder immediately to keep users engaged.

    #2 Practice anywhere and anytime

    Before entering the VR environment, ask users to enter practice time and connect to the heart rate sensor. The interaction is much easier to finish on a 2D interface than in a 3D VR environment. The graph shows real-time heart rate, users aim to increase heart rate variability, which is to make the heart rate wave close to a sine wave. The deeper the wave is, the closer users reach the ideal status.
    After the practice, users can jog down their feelings and review the session analysis to keep on track of the practice.

    #3 Track and measure progress

    Provide data insights and analysis for a period and detailed analysis for every session. Encouraging gadgets give users a sense of fulfilment. Chronic pain treatment is a long progress, data tracking will record users progress to motivate them to keep on track.
