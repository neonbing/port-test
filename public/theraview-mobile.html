WORK ABOUT

Chronic migraine puts a lot of pressure and anxiety on patients, so patients are desperate for ways to overcome it. TheraView provides at-home daily VR-based biofeedback therapy to alleviate pain in the long term and also aims to motivate patients to keep pain management.

Time:

January 2021 - February 2021

Role:

Mobie UX design, User research and User Evaluation

Team:

This is a sole design project followed a group project that I participated in with Frank Zhou and Robin Yang

Problem

Biofeedback is an effective way to prevent migraines, but patients lack easy access to it and hard to keep practicing it frequently

People who suffer from chronic migraine can’t manage their normal daily life and lose the ability to work or live normally. Pain killer doesn’t always help and preventive medication costs a patient over 6000 dollars a year. We developed an effective preventive non-medicine treatment, VR-biofeedback using Oculus Go VR headset.

In the 8-week study with 50 chronic migraine patients in randomized groups using this device daily, we found that people may forget to practice or lose motivation. And people want to keep using it but are reluctant to buy VR headsets just for this therapy. So how might we make VR-biofeedback therapy more ubiquitous and motive patients to keep practicing?

Solution

Engaging users in the onboarding session with easy-to-digest information bite and questions

Most users may not understand what biofeedback is, so the onboarding process introduces biofeedback and uses questions to make users familiarize the component of biofeedback therapy.

Thorough tutorial to cover all steps in the process to reduce uneasiness

The tutorial goes through the whole process again even though the introduction already exists in the process. For IoT projects, users are easier to feel lost and confused, so it’s important to provide help at every stage.

Attracting and immersive home page to attract users to start and easy to follow setup with hardwares

Full-size short video clip aligns with the immersive VR environment and attracts users attention. Users can set up the device after onboarding. Further setup is after tapping the start button, which reduces the resistance of start.

Biofeedback therapy and timely feedback and data log to strengthen users’ belief

The minimalized graph shows real-time heart rate. In biofeedback therapy, users aim to increase heart rate variability, which is to make the heart rate wave close to a sine wave.
After the practice, users can review the session analysis and jog down feelings and notes for future reference.

Interpret the complicated terms and data in plain words for users, so they will fully recognize the progress

In addition to the graph, highlight two major indicators in words for glance. In addition, analyze and explain the meaning behind the data so that users without background knowledge can understand.

USER RESEARCH methodologies

Understand user needs and narrow key themes for product exploration

Key Themes

  • Coping with chronic migraine is a long journey, which needs a lot of patience, encouragement and confidence.

  • There are 5 key factors shown in our research that make patients fail on a daily treatment
      • Don’t understand or trust the therapy
      Hard to access, e.g. need to receive at clinic
      Don’t have a fixed same time to receive
      Don’t have timely feedback
      Forget or hard to tell progress through their own memory

  • These insights can be translated into actionable product design

    Prototype and User Test

    I tested the prototype with 3 users, using the usability test with think aloud protocol to go through the whole process. The main research problems are: How easy is it to learn biofeedback technique, the practice procedure and meaning of data? How useful is the tracking data? How do users use their tracking data?
    There are three key findings from the research

  • Users feel lost in the onboarding setup because of lack of context and explanation. Thus, I break down the whole setup procedure into chunks and provide enough information for users to make the choice and always provide the “come back later” option.

  • It takes a while for users to understand the tracking data graph and they won’t go too deep into figuring it out. Thus, I add glanceable reviews for users to quickly digest.

  • Users are very familiar with standard interfaces so they always take a glance and then take actions quickly rather than reading every word on the interface. Thus, I provide important information more than once in the process and avoid adding interfaces after actions.

    Future Steps

    Biofeedback is proved to relieve a variety of pain and disorders like anxiety, ADHD and low back pain. In the future, we could research into what other groups of people need and adjust our product to suit their needs and help them.
